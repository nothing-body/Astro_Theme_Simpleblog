## Reviews and Reputation

The reputation signal is mixed.

On [G2](https://www.g2.com/products/proxy-seller/reviews), Proxy-Seller was listed at 4.8/5 from 110 reviews when checked. G2's product summary describes residential, static ISP, datacenter IPv4/IPv6, and mobile proxies, and the visible review summary says users often praise support, setup, and proxy quality, while one limitation mentioned is lack of a free trial.

On [Trustpilot](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/proxy-seller.com), Proxy-Seller's rating was unavailable when checked because Trustpilot displayed a guideline-breach warning. The same page still showed 747 reviews, with 72% 5-star and 20% 1-star, and said Trustpilot had removed a number of fake reviews for the company.

[Reviews.io](https://www.reviews.io/company-reviews/store/proxy-seller.com) showed 4.8 from 25 reviews and 96% reviewer recommendation when checked. This is a much smaller sample than Trustpilot or G2, so it should be treated as supporting context rather than a decisive reputation score.

Recent Trustpilot negatives complained about unexpected IP changes, support delays, replacement country mismatch, and platform-specific residential/IP classification problems. Proxy-Seller replies generally framed some cases as network infrastructure changes or third-party database classification differences, and asked reviewers to provide account details for investigation.

## Actual User Feedback Themes

The concrete user comments add more nuance than the rating averages. Positive G2-style feedback usually centers on quick setup, useful support, and the convenience of buying specific proxy types or locations from one dashboard. That is the strongest signal for buyers who already know the country, protocol, and proxy category they need.

The negative user comments point to operational risk. Recent Trustpilot examples included users reporting unexpected IP replacement, country mismatch after replacement, slower-than-expected support handling, and residential proxies being classified differently by the target service or third-party IP databases. These comments matter because proxy success often depends on the exact target site, not only on whether the proxy technically connects.

For a practical purchase decision, read user comments by use case. A buyer using Proxy-Seller for a country-specific static IP may care most about replacement location and subnet stability, while a buyer using residential traffic should test whether the target platform classifies the IPs as expected before scaling.

## Refund and Terms Notes

Proxy-Seller's [Return Policy](https://proxy-seller.com/zh/return/) says services are prepaid. It states customers may claim a full refund, excluding fees, within 72 hours in specific cases such as proxy inoperability that cannot be replaced, inability to connect to the IP confirmed by support, lack of the necessary IP location, lack of IP for the selected use, or purchasing for a prohibited purpose. It also says weekly products generally lose refund eligibility after 24 hours, and 3-day trial residential proxies generally are not refundable.

The [Public Offer](https://proxy-seller.com/zh/offer/) says use of the services means accepting the agreement and return policy. It also prohibits certain uses, including accessing services for benchmarking or competitive monitoring without permission.

## Best Fit

Proxy-Seller may be useful if you need many country-specific static proxies, want a mix of IPv4/IPv6/ISP/residential/mobile choices, and value manual location selection and dashboard/API workflows. The long list of country pages makes it especially oriented toward buyers who already know the exact location and proxy type they want.

It is less comfortable for buyers who need fully transparent static pricing before opening the selector, need guaranteed classification on a third-party platform, or cannot tolerate IP replacement or location changes. In those cases, test a small order and verify the IPs against your own target service before scaling.

## Buying Checklist

1. Confirm the exact final checkout price for country, quantity, duration, and proxy type.
2. Ask support whether your target platform is compatible with the proxy type you plan to buy.
3. Check whether the plan is trial, weekly, monthly, or longer term, because refund rules differ.
4. Verify IP classification with multiple databases and your actual target service.
5. Keep proof of location requirements and support promises before paying.
6. Do not rely on Trustpilot rating alone because the page had a guideline-breach warning.
